Cookie Policy

Last updated: 10 July 2026

This policy explains how this website uses cookies and similar technologies. The short version: we keep it to an absolute minimum, and we set no advertising or analytics cookies.

1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website. Similar technologies include local storage and pixels. They can be "first-party" (set by the site you are visiting) or "third-party" (set by another service the site loads).

2. Cookies we set

None. This website does not set any first-party cookies. There is no analytics tracking, no advertising tracking and no marketing pixels on this site.

3. Third-party services we load

To display and run the site, we load a small number of resources from third parties. These requests share your IP address with the provider as a technical necessity, and in limited cases the provider may use its own cookies or storage:

  • Google Fonts — serves the typeface on this site. Google states that font requests are not used for profiling and no cookies are set by the Fonts API.
  • jsDelivr (CDN) — serves the phone-input library used in the signup form. jsDelivr is a content delivery network and does not set cookies.
  • GitHub Pages — hosts this website. GitHub may log requests (such as IP addresses) for security and operational purposes.

4. The signup form

When you join the waitlist, your number is sent directly to our secure form backend. This submission does not set cookies and no tracking identifier is attached to it.

5. Managing cookies

Because we set no cookies, there is nothing to manage on our side. Your browser settings let you block or delete cookies from any site; doing so will not affect how this website works.

6. Changes

If we introduce cookies in future — for example, analytics when the full Ride site launches — we will update this policy first and, where required, ask for your consent before any non-essential cookies are set.

7. Contact

Questions about this policy: privacy@ride.bs. For how we handle personal information more broadly, see our Privacy Policy.
